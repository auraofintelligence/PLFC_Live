🎣 PLFC Digital Meeting System

Point Lookout Fishing Club — Live, Local & Connected

Image: Wide shot of a PLFC meeting setup with dual ultra short throw projections on walls, a TV facing the committee, and members seated around a table.

Overview

The Point Lookout Fishing Club operates a portable, high-impact digital meeting system designed for clarity, speed, and real-world usability.

It enables structured in-person meetings with optional hybrid capability, without relying on complex infrastructure or permanent installations.

Ownership & Governance

All equipment and core system components are privately owned by the Secretary.

Ongoing use, development, and availability of the system are highly dependent on outcomes, committee engagement, and member contribution.

This ensures the system remains:

  • Practical and results-driven
  • Aligned with club needs
  • Sustainable without burdening volunteers

Image: Close-up of Ryzen PC control station on a table with cables neatly routed to projectors and audio system.

Core Control Hub

A dedicated Ryzen PC acts as the central control system for all meetings.

  • Manages all displays
  • Hosts meeting software
  • Handles live document access and research
  • Routes audio through the venue PA

This creates a single, stable operational point with no device switching.

Image: Two ultra short throw projections side-by-side showing agenda on one wall and remote participants on the other.

Visual Environment

The system uses a three-screen layout:

  • Projector A: Agenda and structured meeting flow
  • Projector B: Remote participants or shared content
  • Committee TV: Internal working screen (search, editing, planning)

This creates a layered environment where the audience sees clarity, while the committee has full control.

Image: Table with multiple microphones placed evenly and a wireless handheld microphone being passed to an attendee.

Audio System

Audio is delivered through the Point Lookout Bowls Club PA system.

  • Multiple table microphones for committee discussion
  • Wireless roaming microphone for attendees

All microphones are combined into a single clean feed into the system, ensuring consistent sound in-room.

Operation

Bump-In (≈15 minutes)

  • Set up PC and displays
  • Connect audio to PA
  • Quick system check

Meeting

  • Agenda always visible
  • Committee operates from internal screen
  • Attendees engage via roaming microphone

Bump-Out (≈10 minutes)

  • Disconnect core components
  • Pack and reset space
Image: Clean pack-down scene with cables coiled, gear packed into a crate, and empty meeting room.

Design Principles

  • Single control system
  • Clear and persistent visuals
  • Natural audio flow
  • Fast setup and pack down
  • Scalable for future use

Outcome

A meeting system that is:

  • Clear for attendees
  • Powerful for organisers
  • Practical for volunteers
  • Adaptable for future growth
